low riders


Low Rider , a Los Angeles nickname for ghetto youth. Originally the term was coined to describe the youth who had lowered
the bodies of their cars so that they rode low, close to the ground.

most famous low rider


1964 Chevrolet Impala named "Gypsy Rose," by Jesse Valadez, on display in the Petersen Automotive Museum. It is considered to be one of the most
iconic lowriders ever built.
